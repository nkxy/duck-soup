About Me

Why design?

I've taken a bit of a scenic route on my way to design - starting first with software development, and then moving onto product management. In both cases, there was something missing, which I eventually found in design.

Empathy. I know it's just slightly overused, but I'm deeply passionate about it. Putting yourself in someone else's shoes is not easy, but allows you to gain so much perspective. As a designer, it helps not only with understanding your user, but also with your teammates.

Great collaboration can go miles above what any individual can do. It's a great feeling when you're able to bounce ideas off one another when trying to solve a challenging design problem, or other ideas sparking something in your head.

Growing up, I would look around and imitate what my parents or friends were doing. Monkey see, monkey do. But who was I in all of this? What was I bringing to the table? Design presents a great opportunity for me to be inspired by others, but to also push myself to create something new and uniquely me.

What else I'm involved in

Tech for social good

UW Blueprint builds software products for social good by working closely with our non-profit partners. I worked on a project for Plastics For Change, where we helped reduce plastic pollution in India through a web app for waste pickers. I also had the privilege to lead the organization for two terms as president, where I was able to help our organization grow in our capabilities.

Podcast for systemic thinking

I had a big podcast phase when I started university. During this, I started two podcasts, one of them for Engineers Without Borders. We examined modern issues through a systemic thinking lens. In other words, we talked a lot about weed, Reddit, and the Tims on campus, and how they illustrate systems principles.
